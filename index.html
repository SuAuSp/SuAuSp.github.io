Competing Events Social Network: Social Network Analysis

Jul. 2023 - May 2024

Research Assistant, Undergraduate Research Project and Subsequent Research, USTC

Advisor: Professor Wentao Xu (Department of Communication of Science and Technology, USTC)

Ø  Sought to determine how and to what extent sentiment polarity associates with language toxicity for Twitter users during United States presidential elections.

Ø  Filtered tweets using keywords and hashtags. Analyzed tweets via LIWC+TF-IDF and Vader for sentiment analysis, as well as applied Google Perspective API for toxicity analysis. Visualized the distribution of sentiment and toxicity into 2D and 3D heatmaps. Dividing users into two categories through a retweet network. Statistically compared users in the "hot" areas of heatmaps.

Ø  Found that contrary to the conventional assumption that well-connected users in a social network are more valuable, less-engaged users are much more important than well-connected users in sentiment analysis, as they demonstrated less toxicity with more positive sentiment than the more engaged users.

Ø  Published a paper as the second author on the findings of this research: "Polarized Patterns of Language Toxicity and Sentiment of Debunking Posts on Social Media" on arXiv.
